Frequently Asked Questions About Concrete in Fayetteville

How much does a concrete driveway cost in Fayetteville, NC?

Concrete driveway costs in Fayetteville range from $8-$18 per square foot. A standard 400 sq ft driveway costs $3,200-$7,200. Stamped concrete adds 40-60% to the cost. Factors include thickness (4" standard, 5-6" for heavy vehicles), finish type, and site prep requirements.

What is stamped concrete vs regular concrete?

Stamped concrete has decorative patterns pressed into the surface to mimic brick, stone, slate, or wood. Regular concrete has a smooth or broom finish. Stamped costs $12-$25/sq ft vs $8-$15/sq ft for regular. Both have similar 25-30 year lifespans when properly installed with reinforcement.

How thick should a concrete driveway be?

Residential driveways should be 4 inches thick minimum. For heavier vehicles (trucks, RVs), we recommend 5-6 inches. Commercial driveways need 6-8 inches. All driveways should use 4,000 PSI concrete and rebar or wire mesh reinforcement for optimal strength.

How long does concrete take to cure?

Concrete reaches 70% strength in 7 days and full strength in 28 days. Walk on new concrete after 24-48 hours. Wait 7 days before driving on a new driveway. Avoid heavy vehicles for 28 days. Proper curing (keeping moist) during the first week is critical.

When is the best time to pour concrete in Fayetteville?

Spring (March-May) and fall (September-November) are ideal when temperatures are 50-75°F. Summer pours require early morning starts and rapid curing precautions. Winter pours are possible above 40°F with blankets. North Carolina's mild climate allows year-round work with proper planning.

Do I need a permit for concrete work in Fayetteville?

Most concrete work requires a permit from the City of Fayetteville Permitting Department. This includes driveways, foundations, and slabs over 120 sq ft. We handle all permitting for our customers at no extra charge.

How long does a concrete driveway last in North Carolina?

A properly installed concrete driveway lasts 25-30 years on average in North Carolina. With sealing every 2-3 years, some last 50+ years. NC's mild climate (fewer freeze-thaw cycles than northern states) is ideal for concrete longevity. Quality installation is the #1 factor.

What causes concrete to crack?

Concrete cracks from: shrinkage during curing, settling of improperly compacted base, heavy loads, tree roots, and temperature changes. Prevention includes: control joints every 8-10 feet, proper base compaction, adequate thickness, rebar reinforcement, and correct water-cement ratio. Hairline cracks under 1/8" are normal.
